Dish Description
Litti Chokha This is arguably the most iconic dish of Bihar. Litti are small, round balls made from whole wheat flour and stuffed with a mixture of sattu (roasted gram flour), spices, and herbs..
Khaja Khaja is a sweet, crispy, and flaky pastry, popular in Bihar and other parts of eastern India. It's made from a dough of wheat flour, sugar, and mawa (milk solids), and then deep-fried until it turns golden brown and crispy. The pastry is then dipped in sugar syrup, adding an extra layer of sweetness.
Makhana Makhana, also known as fox nut or lotus seeds, has been a staple in Indian diets for centuries. Traditionally cultivated in Bihar's Mithila region, this nutrient-dense seed is now gaining global recognition as a superfood.
